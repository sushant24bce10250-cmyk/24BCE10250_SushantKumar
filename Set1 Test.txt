Q1)List the name of each employee along with their job title and department name.

Ans:SELECT e.name, j.job_title, d.dept_name FROM Employees e JOIN Jobs j ON e.job_id = j.job_id JOIN Departments d ON e.dept_id = d.dept_id;

Q2)Find all employees whose salary is higher than the average salary of the 'IT' department.

Ans:SELECT name, salary FROM Employees WHERE salary >(SELECT AVG(salary) FROM Employees WHERE dept_id =(SELECT dept_id FROM Departments
WHERE dept_name = 'IT'));

Q3)Calculate the total salary expenditure for each department name (not ID).

Ans:SELECT d.dept_name, SUM(e.salary) AS total_salary FROM Employees e JOIN Departments d ON e.dept_id = d.dept_id GROUP BY d.dept_name;

Q4)Display departments that have more than 2 employees and an average salary above 70,000.

Ans:SELECT d.dept_name,COUNT(e.emp_id) AS total_employees,AVG(e.salary) AS avg_salary FROM Employees e JOIN Departments d ON e.dept_id = d.dept_id GROUP BY d.dept_name HAVING COUNT(e.emp_id) > 2 AND AVG(e.salary) > 70000;

Q5)Retrieve a list of employee names and their respective manager's names.

Ans:SELECT e.name AS employee,m.name AS manager FROM Employees e LEFT JOIN Employees m ON e.manager_id = m.emp_id;

Q6)Find the top 3 highest-paid employees and list them in descending order.

Ans:SELECT name, salary FROM Employees ORDER BY salary DESC LIMIT 3;

Q7)Find all Developers working in the 'San Francisco' location.

Ans:SELECT e.name FROM Employees e JOIN Jobs j ON e.job_id = j.job_id JOIN Departments d ON e.dept_id = d.dept_id WHERE j.job_title = 'Developer'AND d.location = 'San Francisco';

Q8)Find the number of employees hired each year

Ans:SELECT YEAR(hire_date) AS hire_year,COUNT(*) AS total_employees FROM Employees GROUP BY YEAR(hire_date);

Q9)List job titles where the maximum salary of an employee in that role is less than 100,000.

Ans:SELECT j.job_title,MAX(e.salary) AS max_salary FROM Employees e JOIN Jobs j ON e.job_id = j.job_id GROUP BY j.job_title HAVING MAX(e.salary) < 100000;

Q10)Find the names of employees who work in the same department as 'David'.

Ans:SELECT name FROM Employees WHERE dept_id =(SELECT dept_id FROM Employees WHERE name = 'David');

Q11)Show all department names even if they have no employees assigned to them.

Ans:SELECT d.dept_name, e.name FROM Departments d LEFT JOIN Employees e ON d.dept_id = e.dept_id;

Q12)Find employees who were hired between 2018 and 2020 and earn more than 60,000.

Ans:SELECT name, salary, hire_date FROM Employees WHERE hire_date BETWEEN '2018-01-01' AND '2020-12-31' AND salary > 60000;

Q13)Find employees who earn more than the average salary of their own department.

Ans:SELECT e.name, e.salary, e.dept_id FROM Employees e WHERE e.salary >(SELECT AVG(salary) FROM Employees WHERE dept_id = e.dept_id);

Q14)Find the job title and salary of the employee with ID 504.

Ans:SELECT e.name, j.job_title, e.salary FROM Employees e JOIN Jobs j ON e.job_id = j.job_id WHERE e.emp_id = 504;

Q15)For each manager (by ID), find the minimum salary of the people reporting to them.

Ans:SELECT manager_id,MIN(salary) AS min_salary FROM Employees WHERE manager_id IS NOT NULL GROUP BY manager_id;

Q16)List departments that do not have any 'Analysts'.

Ans:SELECT dept_name FROM Departments WHERE dept_id NOT IN (SELECT DISTINCT e.dept_id FROM Employees e JOIN Jobs j ON e.job_id = j.job_id WHERE j.job_title = 'Analyst');

Q17)Find the difference between the highest and lowest salary in the company.

Ans:SELECT MAX(salary) - MIN(salary) AS salary_difference FROM Employees;

Q18)Display employee names and hire dates, sorted by department name (A-Z) and then by salary (High to Low).

Ans:SELECT e.name,e.hire_date,d.dept_name,e.salary FROM Employees e JOIN Departments d ON e.dept_id = d.dept_id ORDER BY d.dept_name ASC, e.salary DESC;
